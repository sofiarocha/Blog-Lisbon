Panteão Nacional

This is one of the most iconic monuments, not only of Lisbon, but also of Portugal. Here are the tombs of the nation symbols, the most important figures of the country. If you visit Panteão Nacional, you will see the grave of figures from areas as different as football, fado or politics, such as Eusébio da Silva Ferreira, football player from the national team, Amália Rodrigues, the gratest fado singer of all times or Manuel de Arriaga, the first President of the Portuguese Republic.

This is the place to honour the names that helped to build the country, with different contributions to the portuguese identity, who distinguished themselves at the service of the country.

The building was an idea of Passos Manuel, minister of Portugal, who declares in 1836 the construction of a place to honour whoever that stood up for the nation. Initially built in the second half of XVI century, the monument was totally rebuilt and finished in late XVII century by the hand of the architect João Antunes. The building work took so long that the portuguese created a saying based on the history of Panteão. The building has the official name of Santa Engrácia, so when the portuguese want to say that a construction is taking too long to finish they say that is a construction of 'Santa Engrácia'.

Take your time to visit the monument inside and go up to the balcony with an extraordinary view to the city and the Tagus river. Enjoy the surroundings with the typical neighbourhoods of Alfama e Graça. Don't miss the 'miradouros' and go for a ride in the 28 tram. If you visit the site in the mornings of saturday aor monday you will find one of the most famous street markets of the country. The 'Feira da Ladra' is know to be the place where you can find anything. Just give it a try. Anything you can remeber of!
