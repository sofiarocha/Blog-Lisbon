Where to Go

Decide where do you want to go and we guide you!

Augusta Street Arch

Climb up one of Lisbon’s iconic buildings for a unique view of the city.
Oceanary

Considered the best aquarium in the world by Tripadvisor, in 2015 and 2017, Oceanário de Lisboa creates emotions through the oceans and its 8,000 marine creatures.
Jerónimos Monastery

The Jerónimos Monastery was proclaimed a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983. This notable 16th century work of architecture became part of Portuguese identity and culture.
Elevadores

Take a ride on one of Lisbon’s most iconic lifts (elevadores): Elevador da Glória, Elevador da Bica or Elevador de Santa Justa.
25 de Abril Bridge

The bridge is 2,300 m long, with 1,013 m comprising the central suspended span. Nowadays is posible to climb the lift and enjoy the view over this beautiful city.
Trams

Operating in Lisbon since 1879. Nowadays the most famous routes are the tram 28 from Martim Moniz Square to Graça and the 15 by the river.
Belém Tower

UNESCO World Heritage, a cornerstone of Portuguese identity and an icon of a country historically moulded by its proximity to the ocean and its maritime discoveries of new worlds.
Terreiro do Paço

One of Lisbon’s iconic squares that you must see. This famous square is ready to welcome you with various attractions and a nobility that is clear to see.
São Jorge Castle

Built in the mid-11th century, this fortification still retains eleven towers and one of the most amazing view over the city.
Eduardo VII Park

From Parque Eduardo VII you have a stunning vista of the park, imposing statue of the Marquis of Pombal and the Avenida da Liberdade, you can see the Tagus, the south bank of the river and, on a good day, the hills of Arrábida.
Sé Cathedral

Lisbon cathedral from the 13th century is one of the city’s most iconic buildings and its most important church.
National Pantheon

The National Pantheon – or church of Santa Engrácia – is a grand Baroque building housing the tombs of the country’s most distinguished figures.
